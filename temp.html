Latitude vs. Temperature

As expected, the temperatures are higher nearer to the equator, the middle of the graph and lower nearest to poles, the left and right ends of the graph. This makes sense when you think of climates worldwide. In general, you see temperatures more tropical nearest the equator, but you will see colder temperatures the further you move from the equator. One of the odd things that I noticed, is that it seems like there smaller proportion of cities to the south of the equator than north of the equator. As a result, the data is sort of cutoff, towards the southern end.
